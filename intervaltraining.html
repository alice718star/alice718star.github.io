Interval Training

Interval training is alternating periods of high-intensity exercise with periods of low-intensity exercise or recovery periods.

It is an effective way to train as it helps you burn more calories, builds your endurance, and makes your workouts more entertaining.

Depending on the session, some classes use a timer for each workout such as doing 30 seconds of burpees before switching to weights.

Tips for Interval Training

  • Remember to stretch and warm up so you can prep your body for action.
  • Drink water as needed between workouts.
  • Keep track of your reps and weights. If you are looking to improve, it is a good idea to note how many reps you do so that the next time, you can aim to increase the number of reps. If lifting at a certain weight compromises your form, go lower then in the next time, aim to go back up to that weight.
  • Workout buddies not only can motivate you to exercise, but there are workouts that require a partner such as throwing a weighted ball.
