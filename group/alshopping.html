Shopping in NYC

Fifth Avenue

In New York City, there is no shortage of yellow cabs that would take you to Fifth Avenue, where you will take in the sights of colorful window displays of grand department stores. Take a break at the world-famous Rockefeller Center, where you can munch on a variety of foods and continue your shopping adventures underground.

Herald Square

Herald Square is home to the Macy's flagship store, where in addition to thematic window displays, you will find more than 10 levels of fashion and home furnishings. Don't worry, there are escalators and elevators! Nearby lies a plethora of brand-name clothing stores like Gap, Old Navy, as well as Koreatown where you can try out Korean food to your heart's delight.

Soho

Unique in its offering of upscale boutiques, chain stores, and art galleries, Soho in Lower Manhattan offers a cultural experience to visitors who will get to see the architectural style of the buildings as they stroll along the cobbled streets.

Store recommendations: Birchbox, Evolution, Housing Works Bookstore Cafe, Jacque Torres Chocolate, MoMa Design Store, Topshop

World Trade Center

The rebuilt World Trade Center site houses some of the most marvelous structural achievements such as the Oculus, a train station, plaza and the shopping mall (called Westfield World Trade Center) in one, shaped like a pair of wings. Nearby is Brookfield Place, a luxury shopping destination that includes Hermés and Salvatore Ferragamo.

Westfield World Trade Center includes: Michael Kors, Hugo Boss, John Varvatos and Turnbull & Asser.

Brookfield Place includes: Saks Fifth Avenue,Hermés, Salvatore Ferragamo and Ermenegildo Zegna, Theory, Michael Kors, Diane von Furstenberg, Vince, and Paul Smith.
